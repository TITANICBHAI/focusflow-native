Why Standard App Blockers Fail for ADHD

Most focus apps are designed for neurotypical users who are easily distracted but have intact impulse control. For people with ADHD, the problem is different: the impulse to open Instagram isn't a lapse in attention — it's an executive function failure that's neurologically faster than the rational decision to stop it.

ADHD is associated with reduced activity in the prefrontal cortex — the circuits responsible for inhibitory control. When the impulse to check a phone fires, it often fires and completes before the "don't do this" signal catches up. This is why telling someone with ADHD to "just use willpower" is genuinely ineffective.

What "Hard Enforcement" Actually Means

  • The app is physically blocked at the OS level, not just visually indicated
  • The block cannot be removed with a single tap
  • The blocker itself cannot be uninstalled without a deliberate multi-step process
  • A reboot doesn't end the session
