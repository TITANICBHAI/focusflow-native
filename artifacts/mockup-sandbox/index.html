FocusFlow — Screen Time Blocker & Focus App for Android and Desktop (2025)

By TBTechs · Updated May 2025 · Android, Windows, macOS

FocusFlow (by TBTechs) is a hard-enforcement screen time management app for Android, Windows, and macOS. It blocks distracting apps and websites using three stacked enforcement layers: Android Accessibility Service, a local VPN tunnel, and Android Device Administrator mode. FocusFlow offers three distinct blocking modes, a priority-based scheduling engine, and optional aversive feedback — making it one of the hardest-to-bypass focus apps available in 2025.

How FocusFlow Blocks Apps

FocusFlow uses three independent enforcement layers:

  1. Accessibility Service — monitors every app window, fires within milliseconds when a blocked app is detected, and performs up to 5 rapid re-checks at 150ms intervals to catch apps that relaunch themselves.
  2. Local VPN Tunnel — routes selected app traffic into a null-routing tunnel that drops all packets. Can kill all non-emergency network traffic in global mode.
  3. Device Administrator — via FocusDayDeviceAdminReceiver, prevents the app from being uninstalled. System Guard intercepts navigation to Uninstall, Clear Data, and Accessibility Settings screens during active sessions.

FocusFlow Key Features

  • Three blocking modes: Task-Based (whitelist during sessions), Standalone (timed blacklist), Always-On (permanent blacklist)
  • Daily Allowance Engine with three sub-modes: count (limit app opens), time-budget (limit total minutes), interval (limit to defined windows)
  • YouTube Shorts Blocker — closes Shorts player while leaving YouTube accessible
  • Instagram Reels Blocker — targets Reels viewer without blocking the full app
  • Browser keyword/URL blocker — Chrome, Firefox, Samsung Internet, and more
  • Aversive feedback — dims screen to 2% brightness, applies 70% black overlay, pulses vibration on blocked app open
  • System Guard — intercepts Uninstall, Clear Data, and Accessibility Settings navigation during sessions
  • Boot Recovery — BootReceiver restarts blocking service and VPN automatically after device reboot
  • Priority-based scheduling engine with conflict resolution, gap compression, and overrun rebalancing
  • Home screen widget and persistent foreground service notification
  • Cross-platform: Android (8.0+) + Windows (10+) + macOS (11+)
  • Free to use — no subscription required

Frequently Asked Questions

What is FocusFlow app?

FocusFlow (by TBTechs) is a hard-enforcement screen time management app for Android, Windows, and macOS. It blocks distracting apps and websites using Android Accessibility Services, a local VPN, and Device Administrator mode. It is unrelated to other apps sharing the FocusFlow name — TBTechs' version is a hard-enforcement blocking suite, not a simple Pomodoro timer.

How does FocusFlow prevent you from uninstalling it?

FocusFlow requests Android Device Administrator privileges via FocusDayDeviceAdminReceiver. Once granted, Android blocks the standard uninstall path. The System Guard feature also intercepts navigation to the Uninstall, Clear Data, and Accessibility Settings screens during active sessions.

Can FocusFlow block YouTube Shorts without blocking all of YouTube?

Yes. FocusFlow's YouTube Shorts Blocker closes the Shorts player while leaving the main YouTube feed, search, subscriptions, and video playback accessible. Instagram Reels blocking works the same way — targeting the Reels viewer without blocking the full Instagram app.

Does FocusFlow work after a phone reboot?

Yes. FocusFlow includes a BootReceiver that automatically restarts the blocking service and VPN tunnel if the device reboots during an active session, closing the most common bypass method.

How is FocusFlow different from Freedom and Digital Wellbeing?

Freedom uses Accessibility Service and VPN but lacks Device Admin, boot recovery, and aversive feedback. It costs $39.99/year. Digital Wellbeing uses soft timers dismissable with one tap, has no VPN enforcement, and cannot prevent its own uninstallation. FocusFlow combines all three enforcement layers and is free.
